## What Merlin is

Merlin is an **AI co-worker for your buildings** — and like any great colleague, it doesn't just report problems, it handles them. Behind the single, calm interface is a **growing fleet of agents**: **general agents** that handle the broad, cross-cutting work across your building, and **specialized agents** that each go deep on a single concern (cleaning, HVAC, security, cold-chain, compliance, and more). Every one reasons continuously over the real signals coming off your spaces and closes the loop between what's happening and what should be done about it — and the fleet keeps growing as new domains come online.

Adaptiv designs and builds the devices that give Merlin its senses — Smart Displays, sensors, and connected hardware deployed in **more than 8,000 buildings over five years**. That's why your co-worker is credible from day one: it isn't learning your building from a cold start — it's applying years of physical-world judgment, refined across thousands of buildings, to your specific operation. Software-only competitors can build agents; they can't ground them in telemetry from hardware they own. **That's the moat.**

## The magic: decisions, not dashboards

Open Merlin and you don't face a wall of charts. You see one question — **"What needs a decision in the next 30 minutes?"** — and a ranked list of proposals from your co-worker. Each is a plain-language ask with a confidence score and three buttons: **Approve. Modify. Dismiss.** Every action is logged.

> _"This restroom hasn't been cleaned in 18.8 hours, past its 4-hour SLA — the most overdue on the floor. Dispatch the crew?"_ → **Approve.**

Behind that one sentence, Merlin read every device on the floor, compared each space to its agreement, ranked the backlog, and drafted the dispatch. You read a line and tap a button. That's the shift: your co-worker does the noticing, the reasoning, and the drafting — you keep the judgment and the control.

Every agent runs the same loop, continuously: **sense** the world → **reason** with domain knowledge → **decide** what should happen → **act** for real → **measure** the outcome. A dashboard tells you something is wrong. Your Merlin co-worker does something about it, and verifies the result.

## What it does, at a glance

- **Sees the building physically.** Live Smart Displays, people counters, leak detectors, dispenser monitors, radar and machine-vision sensors — Merlin grounds every recommendation in what's actually happening, right now.
- **Acts with a human in the loop.** Autonomous where you allow it, held for approval where it matters. Nothing irreversible happens without a tap — and everything is audited.
- **Holds your contractors accountable.** Contracts, SLAs, proposals, and device-derived performance reports in one place. Merlin measures service providers against the agreements they signed — automatically — and hands you the report.
- **Anticipates.** A rolling forecast flags the breach before it happens: air quality, occupancy load, energy-vs-budget, certification thresholds, wellbeing and compliance risk. You act on tomorrow, not yesterday.
- **Keeps you compliant — and certified.** Merlin holds every space to the rules, regulations, certifications, and wellbeing standards that apply to it, and builds the audit trail as it goes (more below).
- **Answers in plain language.** "Which restrooms are overdue?" "What's driving the energy spike?" Ask your co-worker about any sensor, room, route, or SLA — it answers from your live building.
- **Makes the AI visible.** The Smart Display on the wall shows occupants when a space was last cleaned, by whom, and how it's doing — and takes a tap to request service. Your co-worker isn't hidden in the cloud; it's the handshake between the building and the people inside it.

## Sees it coming — the ANTICIPATE pillar

A great co-worker doesn't wait for the fire alarm — they smell the smoke. **Anticipate** is one of Merlin's five pillars, and it's where continuous sensing turns into genuine foresight. Instead of telling you what already broke, your co-worker projects forward and acts while there's still time to change the outcome:

- **Forecast** — a rolling 12-hour read on what's coming across the building: conditions, load, demand, and the spaces about to need attention.
- **Predictive maintenance** — catches the failure _signature_ before the equipment fails. The compressor drifting toward breakdown, the pump vibrating past baseline — flagged while it's still cheap to fix, not after it takes a floor offline.
- **Savings & energy** — tracks consumption against budget in real time and shows where money is leaking before the bill lands.
- **Wellbeing** — watches the trajectory of what makes a space healthy to occupy — air quality, comfort, cleanliness — and intervenes before it dips.
- **SLA risk** — sees which service agreements are about to breach and prompts the save while the clock still favors you.
- **Compliance drift** — flags a standard slipping before it becomes a violation (more below).

Every one of these is the same loop pointed at the future — **sense the trend, reason about where it leads, decide whether to act now, and do it before the problem arrives.** The payoff: you spend your day on tomorrow's decisions instead of firefighting yesterday's incidents.

## Compliant by default — and audit-ready

Rules change. Regulations stack up. Certifications demand evidence — and wellbeing is no longer optional. Most buildings treat all of it as an annual scramble: a frantic hunt for proof the week before the inspector or the certifier arrives.

Merlin treats compliance as a **continuous state, not an event.** It holds every space to the full set of standards that apply to it — health and hygiene codes, fire and safety regulations, air-quality and occupant-wellbeing thresholds, ESG targets, and certification criteria like WELL, LEED, BREEAM, and ISO — and flags drift the moment it starts, long before it becomes a violation.

And because Merlin acts through devices it owns, **the proof builds itself.** Every clean is verified by NFC tap. Every action is logged. Every report is device-derived, not hand-entered. So the audit trail is always current, always defensible, and always one click away. When the inspector or the certifier shows up, the evidence is already there — and your wellbeing and sustainability claims are backed by data, not declarations.

Your co-worker doesn't just keep the building running. It keeps it **provably in line with everything it's held to.**
